Hideo Kojima Returns to His Post-Apocalyptic Universe

In a whirlwind of excitement for fans of the acclaimed Japanese video game creator Hideo Kojima, news has surfaced that he is returning to his uniquely strange post-apocalyptic world. This comeback promises thrilling new adventures and innovative gameplay experiences that only Kojima can deliver, setting expectations high among gamers worldwide.

Legal Dismissal of The Tetris Effect Lawsuit

The legal battle over the adaptation rights to "The Tetris Effect: The Game That Hypnotized the World," a book by former Gizmodo editor Dan Ackerman, has seen its first significant turn. Ackerman's lawsuit against Apple and other entities, alleging they ripped off his work for their 723 film, has been dismissed in court. Judge Katherine Failla ruled that the movie isn't substantially similar to the book, dismissing the claim of misappropriation.

Lena Raine Shares Music from Cancelled Game

The talented composer Lena Raine, best known for her work on Celeste's soundtrack, recently shared some tracks from a cancelled project called Earthblade. Although fans can't play this game in its intended form, the music captures the essence of what could have been an extraordinary gaming experience.

Bandcamp Fridays Return

Gaming enthusiasts and music lovers alike received good news as Bandcamp announced that their popular Bandcamp Fridays will be back for 2025. This initiative, which offers a percentage of revenue to artists during the first Friday of every month, has been instrumental in supporting independent musicians. The Story Behind Balatro's Development

Developer LocalThunk recently unveiled an interesting story behind their game project named Balatro. Initially titled “CardGame,” the production working folder for Balatro hints at its early conception as a card game before evolving into something more complex and engaging.

Pokémon Mascots Come to Life

The Pokémon universe continues to expand beyond digital screens with the unveiling of life-size Koraidon and Miraidon prototypes. These legendary mascasts from Pokémon Scarlet and Violet are not just static displays; their legs can move, imitating how they run in-game. The bike version of Miraidon is also under development, promising a leisurely ride around this summer.

March 2025 Switch Online Game Releases

Nintendo fans are gearing up for the next round of retro games becoming available on Nintendo Switch Online. March’s lineup includes "Donkey Kong" and "Mario's Picross," bringing classic titles to modern gaming platforms.
