New games, old favorites, and a rise of gaming on Netflix

Published on 04 Mar, 2025
Photo by Francesco Ungaro via Pexels

As we dive into this week's gaming news, it's clear that there are plenty of exciting developments happening across the industry. From new game announcements to updates on existing titles, there's something for every type of gamer out there.

The Rise of Suikoden Star Leap

One of the biggest stories in gaming right now is the announcement of Suikoden Star Leap, the first new mainline entry in the series in nearly 20 years. However, fans are less than thrilled with the news that this game will be a gacha title.

The decision to make Suikoden Star Leap a gacha game has sparked controversy among fans, who are worried about the potential for microtransactions and loot boxes. While some may see it as an opportunity to engage new players in the series, others are concerned that it could detract from the core gaming experience.

On the other hand, Suikoden I & II HD remasters are set to launch on March 6th, which is sure to bring excitement for fans of the classic JRPG series. The games have been updated with new features and improvements, making them a great option for both old and new players.

Palworld Now Available on Mac

Palworld, the Pokémon-like game with a twist, has finally made its way to Macs. According to developer Pocketpair, the new version is "optimized to run natively on Macs with Apple silicon," making it easier for players to access the game on their preferred platform.

This is an exciting development, especially since Palworld was initially released as a PC-only title. The addition of Mac support opens up new possibilities for players and developers alike, and could potentially pave the way for future titles in the series.

A New Era for Gaming on Netflix

Netflix has been steadily pushing its gaming efforts towards tie-ins with its streaming offerings, and the latest example is Kid Cosmo, a puzzle game set to launch on March 18th. This marks the second big gaming title to be announced by Netflix in recent months.

The Electric State, another upcoming Netflix game, will premiere just days after Kid Cosmo launches. While details are still scarce about these games, it's clear that Netflix is committed to expanding its gaming offerings and reaching new audiences through its streaming platform.

Rockstar Acquires Video Games Deluxe

Rockstar Games has acquired Video Games Deluxe, a Sydney-based studio that previously partnered with Rockstar on re-releases of LA Noire and the GTA trilogy. The acquisition marks an important milestone in Rockstar's expansion plans.

The studio will now be known as Rockstar Australia, and while no details have been confirmed about upcoming projects, fans are eagerly anticipating what the team might work on next. With Rockstar's reputation for delivering high-quality games, it's likely that we'll see some exciting developments from this new partnership.

A Solid Card for 1440p Gaming

If you're looking to upgrade your gaming rig with a solid card for 1440p play, the RTX 5070 is definitely worth considering. With its powerful specifications and optimized performance, it's an excellent choice for gamers who want to take their experience to the next level.

However, as with any new hardware, there are some potential drawbacks to consider. For example, the RTX 5070 may require significant system upgrades to run smoothly, which could be a barrier for some players.

Mastering Monster Hunter Wilds

For those who have struggled through the initial hours of Monster Hunter Wilds, Polygon's excellent beginner's guide is an absolute must-read. With its comprehensive coverage of the game's mechanics and systems, this guide will help you navigate even the most challenging aspects of the game.

As I mentioned earlier, Wilds can be a daunting experience for new players, but with the right guidance, it's definitely possible to get started and have a blast. So why not check out the guide and start your Wilds journey today?
