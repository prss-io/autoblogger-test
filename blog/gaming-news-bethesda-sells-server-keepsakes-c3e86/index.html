Gaming News: Bethesda Sells Server Keepsakes

15 Apr, 2025
Photo by Mikhail Nilov via Pexels

Bethesda Celebrates 10 Years of The Elder Scrolls Online

In celebration of its 10-year anniversary, Bethesda is selling chunks of the original servers that brought us The Elder Scrolls Online in 2014. Dubbed “The Elder Scrolls Online 10-year Anniversary Server Keepsake,” these are sticks of RAM encased in wood-framed shadowboxes with a zinc alloy commemorative plate.

Each stick is individually numbered and comes at a price tag of $110, but for die-hard fans, it's an opportunity to own a piece of gaming history. With only 2,000 available, these keepsakes are sure to be snapped up quickly by collectors and enthusiasts alike.

The Future of Gaming: What to Expect in the Coming Years

As the video game industry continues to evolve, we can expect even more innovative features and technologies to hit the market. From cloud gaming to virtual reality experiences, there's no shortage of exciting developments on the horizon.

One area that's gaining traction is cross-platform play, which would allow gamers to play together across different devices and platforms. While it may seem like a small step, this could have a significant impact on the way we experience gaming in the future.

The Impact of Gaming on Pop Culture

Gaming has long been a source of entertainment for millions of people around the world, but its influence extends far beyond the screen. From movies and TV shows to music and art, gaming has become an integral part of pop culture.

Games like Fortnite and League of Legends have spawned their own fashion trends and merchandise lines, while others have inspired books, comics, and even theme park attractions. It's clear that gaming is no longer just a hobby, but a driving force behind creativity and innovation in the entertainment industry.

The Gaming Industry Continues to Grow

With the rise of digital storefronts like Steam and the Epic Games Store, it's easier than ever for developers to bring their games to market. This has led to a surge in new releases and indie titles, giving gamers more choice than ever before.

The growth of the gaming industry is also reflected in its economic impact. With revenues projected to reach $190 billion by 2025, it's clear that gaming is no longer just a niche interest, but a major player in the global economy.
