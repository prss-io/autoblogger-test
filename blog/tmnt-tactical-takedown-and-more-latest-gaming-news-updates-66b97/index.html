TMNT Tactical Takedown and More: Latest Gaming News Updates

As a gamer, it's always exciting to hear about the latest developments in the gaming industry. From new game releases to innovative technology advancements, there's always something new and interesting happening. For more updates on the latest gaming news, be sure to check out our Gaming Industry Buzz post.

New Games Coming Soon: TMNT Tactical Takedown Launch Date Revealed

The upcoming turn-based TMNT game, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Tactical Takedown, has finally revealed its launch date. According to developer Strange Scaffold, the game will be available on May 22nd, giving fans of the franchise even more reason to get excited.

Activision Shares Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 3 + 4 Remake Soundtrack

Activision has shared some of the songs on the soundtrack for the upcoming Tony Hawk’s Pro Skater 3 + 4 remake. The game, which launches in July, promises to bring back the classic skateboarding experience with new features and gameplay mechanics. You can find more information about upcoming gaming releases in our Gaming Update Central post.

Xbox Play Anywhere Milestone Reached: Over 1,000 Games Now Available

Microsoft has reached a significant milestone in its Xbox Play Anywhere initiative, with over 1,000 games now available for cross-play between consoles and PCs. This move is seen as a major step towards the company's goal of merging Xbox and Windows.

Nintendo Opens Second US Retail Location in San Francisco

The gaming giant has opened its second retail location in the US, offering fans a chance to experience the latest Nintendo games and consoles firsthand. Located in San Francisco’s Union Square, the store promises to be a must-visit destination for gamers.

Nvidia Reveals Half-Life 2 RTX Remix Demo

Nvidia has announced that it will release a two-hour demo for Half-Life 2 owners, showcasing the capabilities of its RTX 50 cards. The demo promises to bring stunning visuals and improved performance to PC gamers.

RTX 50-Series GPUs Sell Faster than Expected, Say Nvidia

Nvidia has revealed that it has sold more RTX 50-series GPUs in the first five weeks of launch compared to its previous generation. The company attributes this success to the popularity of its new DLSS technology and improved performance.

Samsung Offers Galaxy Store Developers Better Revenue Share Model

As part of its efforts to improve the gaming experience on Android devices, Samsung has announced a new revenue share model for developers using its Galaxy Store. The company promises to offer an 80/20 split, giving developers more control over their earnings.
