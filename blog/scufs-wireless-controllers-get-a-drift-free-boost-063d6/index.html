Scuf's Wireless Controllers Get a Drift-Free Boost

29 May, 2025
Photo by Kyle Loftus via Pexels

Scuf is finally catching up with the times by adding drift-resistant Hall effect sticks to some of its wireless controllers. The updated versions of the Envision and Reflex lines now include the $149.99 Envision, $199.99 Envision Pro for PC, $249.99 Reflex Pro, and $279.99 Reflex FPS for PlayStation 5 and PC.

The Hall effect upgrade brings Scuf's wireless offerings in line with its wired-only Valor Pro and Nomad mobile controllers, replacing the original Reflex and Envision pads that first debuted in 2021 and 2023, respectively. This move is significant as it aligns Scuf with other brands like 8BitDo, Razer, and GameSir, which have already adopted this technology.

It's worth noting that some controller makers are now moving on to a more power-efficient drift-resistant technology: TMR sticks. While Scuf controllers may be pricier than premium options from Microsoft and Sony, they're still popular among gamers due to their unique features like rear paddles and clicky hair-trigger options.

The addition of Hall effect sticks to Scuf's wireless controllers is a welcome upgrade for gamers who value drift-resistance. With this update, Scuf's offerings are now more competitive in the market, making them a great choice for those looking for premium gaming experience.

What Does This Mean for Gamers?

The inclusion of Hall effect sticks in Scuf's wireless controllers is a significant development for gamers. It means that these controllers will be less prone to drift and provide a more precise gaming experience. This upgrade also makes Scuf's offerings more competitive in the market, which can only be a good thing for gamers.

Online Gaming Updates

The addition of Hall effect sticks is not the only notable development in the world of gaming. Other brands are also working on improving their controllers with similar technology. It will be interesting to see how this development shapes the gaming industry in the coming months and years.
